Background About Each Island:

Oahu:

  1. One of the main volcanic origins of Hawaii.
  2. Third Largest Hawaiian island.
  3. Holds the greatest population out of the Hawaiian islands.
  4. Site of Pearl Harbor

Maui:

  1. Maui takes its name from a Polynesian demigod. It was created by two volcanoes, Puu Kukui and Haleakala.
  2. Contains a humpback whale sanctuary.
  3. Known for its tourism attractions and beaches.

Kauai:

  1. Kauai is Hawaii's fourth largest island and is sometimes called the "Garden Island"
  2. Centuries of growth have formed tropical rainforests, forking rivers and cascading waterfalls.
  3. You can kayak the Wailua River, snorkel on Poipu Beach, hike the trails of Kokee State Park, or go ziplining above Kauai's lush valleys.

Molokai:

  1. One and a half million years ago, two large volcanoes pushed through the surface of the Pacific Ocean and created the island of Molokai.
  2. Nearly 40% are of Hawaiian descent, thus the nick name, “The Most Hawaiian Island.”
  3. As the Hawaiians had no written language, most of their pre-contact history has come from chants, passed down from generation to generation, which have kept a chronology of events, battles and genealogy.

Lanai:

  1. Its history is a mix of geology, natural history, and the experiences of people who have lived here for close to 1,000 years.
  2. One of the most beautiful natural scenes in Hawaii.
  3. Reportedly the island is named after a young woman of exceptional beauty who lived in the village of Manele.

Hawaii itself:

  1. The Hawaiian Islands were first settled as early as 400 C.E.
  2. Hawaii was characterized by Mark Twain as “the loveliest fleet of islands that lies anchored in any ocean.”
  3. Hawaii is economically vigorous, with diversified agriculture and manufacturing.
